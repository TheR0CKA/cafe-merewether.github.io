Serves: 4 people

Recommended Serving Size: 300g

Skill Level: 4/5

Nutrional Value
Calories Fat (grams) Carbohydrates (grams) Protein (grams)
1106 48.60 122.40 44.30

Catering to dietary needs:

  • High Cholesterol: Use olive oil or sunflower oil instead of normal spray oil

Ingredients

  • 600g (4 cups) plain flour
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 410mL (1 & 2/3 cups) warm water
  • 125mL (1/2 cup) olive oil
  • 1 brown onion, finely chopped
  • 250g lamb mince
  • 1 tablespoon sweet paprika
  • 1 bunch silverbeet or spinach, stems trimmed, finely shredded
  • 200g feta crumbled
  • 100g fresh ricotta
  • Lemon wedges, to serve

Method

  1. Combine the flour and salt in a large bowl. Make a well in the centre. Add the water and 2 tablespoons of the oil and stir to combine.

  2. Turn onto a lightly floured surface and knead for 10 minutes or until smooth and elastic. Place in a bowl and cover with plastic wrap. Set aside for 2 hours to rest.

  3. Meanwhile, heat 1 tablespoon of the remaining oil in a large frying pan over medium heat. Add the onion and cook, stirring, for 5 minutes or until onion softens.

  4. Add the lamb and cook, stirring with a wooden spoon to break up any lumps, for 5 minutes or until mince changes colour. Add the paprika and stir to combine. Remove from heat and set aside to cool.

  5. Add the silverbeet, feta and ricotta to the lamb mixture and stir to combine. Season with salt and pepper.

  6. Preheat a barbecue flatplate on medium. Divide dough into 6 even portions. Roll out a portion on a lightly floured surface to s 20 x 45cm rectangle.

  7. Spoon one-sixth of the lamb mixture over half the dough rectangle, leaving a 3cm border. Fold remaining half over to enclose filling. Repeat with remaining dough and lamb mixture.

  8. Brush gozleme with remaining oil. Cook on barbecue for 5 minutes each side or until golden brown and crisp (Tip: Use a metal spatula to flip the gozleme). Cut into rectangles and serve immediately with lemon wedges.

  9. Enjoy!
